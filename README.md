# The Forester’s Cruising Kit

Description:

The Forester’s Cruising Kit is a mobile application for collectors of forestry antiquities, historians of the timber industry, and preservers of silvicultural memory, dedicated to cataloging the manual instruments used to measure tree height, diameter, and timber volume that have been superseded by digital forestry tools and LIDAR. Systematize your collection of the tools that measured the great woods — from brass Abney levels and specialized clinometers for height to heavy-duty steel diameter tapes and Biltmore sticks for rapid estimation, from increment borers used to extract core samples to cast-iron log scaling rules and timber scribes for marking felled logs. Create a digital archive of forest management by recording the tool type, manufacturer, scale system, era, and the specific timber region each specimen represents. 

## Example Object Fields (Model "Forestry Instrument"):
•	Cruiser Identifier: Unique code (e.g., "TFCK-KEUFFEL-1940-OREGON-047") 
•	Tool Type: Category (clinometer, Abney level, diameter tape, Biltmore stick, increment borer, log rule, timber scribe). 
•	Specific Function: What it measured (total tree height, DBH (Diameter at Breast Height), annual ring count, board foot volume). 
•	Manufacturer and Brand: Company name (Keuffel & Esser, Suunto, Lufkin, Sandvik, Haglöf, Spencer). 
•	Country of Origin: Where it was made (USA, Sweden, Finland, Germany, Japan). 
•	Era of Production: Decade of manufacture (1890s, 1920s, 1940s, 1960s). 
•	Scale System: Measurement units used (International 1/4" Rule, Doyle Rule, Scribner Decimal C, Metric). 
•	Materials: Primary construction (brass, forged steel, aluminum, boxwood, hickory, canvas-reinforced tape). 
•	Operating Principle: Mechanical (gravity-based clinometer), optical (rangefinder), or direct contact (tapes and rules). 
•	Dimensions and Weight: Length of rule or borer, mass of the instrument. 
•	Condition State: Operational, scale legibility, rust/corrosion level, original finish. 
•	Included Accessories: Original leather scabbard, cleaning rods for borers, sharpening stones for scribes. 
•	Markings and Stamps: Government agency stamps (e.g., U.S.F.S.), lumber company initials, or patent dates. 
•	Provenance: Where found (abandoned ranger station, lumber mill estate, vintage tool auction, Pacific Northwest coastal shop).
